My Research Projects

Plasma Diagnostics for a Water Electrolysis Hall-Effect Thruster

Summer 2023

I spent my most recent summer working at the Imperial Plasma Propulsion Lab at Imperial College London to develop a range of in-house plasma diagnostics devices for their 2kW AQUAHET. This is an engineering prototype of a water electrolysis Hall thruster further developed from the WETHET iteration. In particular, I constructed a Langmuir probe and created a test plan outlining key steps for setup, implementation and data collection to characterise the plasma plumes for oxygen and water propellants.

See an outline of the test plan I developed, with some reflections on what I learned over the summer.

NASA Spacecraft Thermal Management Project

Spring 2023 - present

This two-semester project was based on work for the AdVECT project, which looked at designing an integrated heat pipe and radiator system for nuclear propulsion applications. I investigated working fluid options for this intermediate temperature range with regards to toxicity, operating limits, and compatibility with ceramic. I came across ionic liquids as a potential option and led a team investigating the potential of implementing these liquids as a working fluid in the fall.

Here are the slides summarizing my preliminary investigations into working fluids that I presented at the final lab meeting and here is the first-author paper I wrote in the fall.

Eddy-Current Actuation in Foams

January 2022 - April 2023

This was my first in-depth research experience where I worked with Dr. Mason Peck and Katherine Wilson to explore potential applications of eddy current actuators in space. This is a proposed technology that can be used for non-contact manipulation in space such as damping during docking, maintenance and on-orbit manufacturing. Since eddy currents on solid aluminium plates are a well-solved problem, I modelled and experimentally demonstrated eddy current repulsion on aluminium foams which are lower in density but still maintain high structural integrity. I submitted a conference paper and presented my work at an AIAA student conference in April 2023.

Here is the link to the accepted AIAA paper and conference slides.
